Techniques in Genetic Engineering


CRISPR-Cas9: A revolutionary gene-editing tool.
CRISPR: CRISPR stands for Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats. These are short, repetitive DNA sequences
found in the genomes of bacteria and other microorganisms.
Cas9: Cas9 is a type of enzyme that can be programmed
to target specific DNA sequences. It acts like a pair of molecular scissors, capable of cutting DNA at precise locations.

Gene Cloning: Replicating a specific gene.
Gene cloning is the process of replicating a specific gene by isolating it from an organism's DNA and inserting it into a vector,
such as a plasmid or a virus. This allows scientists to produce multiple copies of the gene, which can then be studied or used for various
purposes, such as producing proteins of interest or studying gene function. Gene cloning is a fundamental technique in molecular biology
and biotechnology, enabling researchers to manipulate and study genes in detail.

Gene Therapy: Treating diseases by correcting defective genes.
Gene therapy is a medical approach aimed at treating diseases by correcting defective genes within a patient's cells.
This can be achieved by introducing functional copies of the faulty gene, repairing the existing gene, or modifying the gene's
expression. Gene therapy holds promise for treating a wide range of genetic disorders, including inherited diseases and certain
types of cancers. It represents a cutting-edge area of medical research with the potential to provide personalized and targeted
treatments for various conditions.
