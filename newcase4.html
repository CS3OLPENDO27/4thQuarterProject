Case Studies/Future Prospects


CRISPR Babies: Ethical debate over human embryo editing in China.
The birth of CRISPR babies in China sparked a global ethical debate over human embryo editing.
In late 2018, Chinese scientist He Jiankui announced the birth of twins whose genomes had
been edited using CRISPR-Cas9 technology to confer resistance to HIV. This controversial
experiment raised concerns about the ethical implications of manipulating the human germline,
including potential unforeseen consequences and the creation of "designer babies"
with edited genetic traits. The incident prompted calls for stricter regulations
and guidelines for gene editing research in humans to ensure responsible and ethical use of this
powerful technology. It underscored the importance of balancing scientific advancement
with ethical considerations to uphold principles of safety, equity, and respect for human
dignity in genomic research and healthcare.

quaBounty Salmon: First genetically modified animal approved for consumption.
The AquaBounty Salmon became the first genetically modified animal approved for consumption.
Engineered to grow faster using a growth hormone gene from Chinook salmon and a genetic switch
from ocean pout, these salmon reach market size more rapidly than conventional salmon.
Despite receiving regulatory approval in various countries, AquaBounty Salmon
has sparked debates over safety, environmental impact, and labeling transparency. While proponents
highlight its potential to meet growing food demands sustainably,
critics raise concerns about ecological risks, consumer acceptance, and long-term health effects.
The approval of AquaBounty Salmon marks a milestone in biotechnology and food production,
shaping discussions about genetically modified organisms in the food supply
and regulatory oversight.
